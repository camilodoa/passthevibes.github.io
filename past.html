Kaiit - "Miss Shiney"
Kaiit’s “Miss Shiney” is your new neo-soul jam. Throughout the song, Kaiit builds a metaphor of herself as a diamond.

In the hook she sings: “I got pressure, she calls me diamond on the weekdays”. She paints a picture of herself as Miss Shiney, a diamond who shines bright, yet deals with pressure on a regular basis.

Later, she brings the listener further into her head and details how doubting herself and overthinking are at the root of a lot of her life’s stress, singing: “Now that’s wack, I don’t know how to act, a false reality that I seem to created in my head, to make me feel I’m not deserving of these things I do”.

The lyrics are catchy, and emotional. However, the song really caught my ear because of the beautiful pairing between Kait’s voice, the soulful piano, funky guitar, and simple drum beat. She floats over the instrumental, describing the stresses and pressure she puts on herself, while implying that she’s still a diamond, shining bright.

J Rick - "Gone"
London producer J Rick is back with “Gone”. The song came out Thursday, May 24th and continues to showcase his ability and versatility as a producer. After producing several tracks for rising UK artist Octavian, including his breakout single “Party Here”, J Rick is focusing on his own project, No Retreat, No Surrender. “Gone” is the second single for the project, after “Short”, which came out back in February.

Both tracks are marked by intriguing samples of vocals and tracks layered into fluid, changing, synthy beat. “Gone” starts with a sample from J Ricks late uncle, British boxer Errol Christie, speaking on boxing as one of the only ways for Black people to make it and later flips a handful of lyrics from Blackstreet’s 1994 “Before I Let You Go”, before mixing in vocals from a viral clip of a sermon from Bishop Dag Heward-Mills. The samples work perfectly with a beat that is always changing, yet keeping the same central themes and elements.

The music video is equally as exciting. The video adds to the feel of “Gone”, pairing the audio with a montage of clips of J Rick adventuring, but also the wide variety of other people, places, and sights that were a part of his trip to Egypt. The track and the video stand out as another creative expression from a talented producer, trying to showcase his own individual artistry as opposed to taking a backseat to a vocalist. “Gone” is a funky, experimental track that adds to the growing hype around J Rick, and his upcoming project.

Xanman - "Hell Yeaa"
Ever since his release back in May, rising DMV rapper Xanman has been releasing song after song. He started with "Pink" and his collab with YungManny “First Day Out”, and then released the Big Plug N*gga mixtape.

His most recent track, is “Hell Yea”, the intro to his upcoming mixtape, Hell Yeah. For the past year and a half, Xanman has shown off his singing as well as his ability to craft great punchlines. “Hell Yea” is all bars. Xanman shows off his energy over a menacing beat and spits several catchy lines.

Some of the bars that immediately caught my ear were in his first verse when he raps: “I grab the choppa, You better lay there, Walk in his house with the Drake, Take Care”. He shortens drako (mini AK-47) to Drake, and then uses the second half of the line to warn anyone he approaches when armed by referencing Drake’s 2011 album, Take Care.

“Hell Yeaa” is a hard-hitting Xanman song full of energy and clever punchlines. Xanman released the song as a music video, which showcases his personality while he raps and dances around with a posse (and some random people in costumes) in New York City.

Goldlink - "Tiff Freestyle"
After last July’s set of 3 concerts on U St. in D.C., Goldlink thanked the fans from his hometown, and let them know that his next project would be distinct from all of his previous work. Just under a year later, he dropped Diaspora, a proud celebration of Black music and culture with a variety of features hailing from all over the world.

On Tiff Freestyle, one of the “featureless” songs, Goldlink’s verse is introduced and followed by the popular British DJ Tiffany Calver. Tiff does her best American accent hyping up Goldlink, which is actually pretty convincing.

Goldlink’s verse, is an honest reflection on his journey done over a smooth piano instrumental. He starts by discussing his aspirations to make money and provide for his future children. Goldlink then reminisces about hot and hectic DC summers, and talks about his come-up.

He saves his best bars for last, ending the freestyle highlighting his current lavish lifestyle through a few major references to Michael Jackson, Prince and Michael Vick. He raps: “I live a life like Michael and keep it purple like Prince, Keep like 7 with me, got n*ggas callin’ me Vick”.

“Tiff Freestyle” is a Goldlink track that may not get as much attention as some of the other tracks on Diaspora that pack major features, but it is another very strong track from the DC rapper.

Bakar - "Hell & Back"
The July 4th episode of Kendall Jenner and Daniel Chetrit’s ZAZA WORLD RADIO on Apple Music featured a premier of an unreleased track from rising UK artist Bakar. The singer/rapper hailing from Camden, London released his debut album Badkid back in May 2018, attracting attention in the UK and around the world.

If you haven’t heard of Bakar, Badkid is an amazing album, highlighting his versatility and influences from classic rock to alternative/indie music to hip-hop. However, Bakar’s newest song, “Hell & Back”, has a completely different feel from his debut album. Bakar stated that he hopes people listen to this track “in the sun”, and the song sounds decidedly warmer and more upbeat than the majority of the songs on Badkid. The track starts with whistling, over a light guitar based instrumental. However, the instrumental is amazingly paired with horns from the Onyx Collective’s Isaiah Barr. Throughout the track, Bakar showcases his unique voice, singing about meeting a lover. One of the lines that immediately stands out is early in the song he sings that “she had green eyes like Mountain Dew”, which creatively paints a picture in the listener’s head.

Bakar cited Camden legend Amy Winehouse as a major influence on “Hell & Back” and his upcoming project Will You Be My Yellow?, which is slated to release on August 23. An official version has not yet been released at the moment, but linked is a rip from ZAZA World Radio’s “THE BEST DAYS OF OUR LIVES” episode. Give this track a listen and keep an eye out for Bakar’s Will You Be My Yellow?.

Nekfeu – "Les Étoiles Vagabondes"
Nekfeu began his career as one of the standout members of 1995, a hip hop collective named for the era of French and American music that they took as inspiration. Since the group’s 2011 LP Paris Sud Minute, Nekfeu has risen to superstardom in his native France, appearing in films and releasing dance-ready hits that endeared him to the broader public, with the help of a slow transition of his appearance from crate digger to hot celebrity.

His new album, Les Étoiles Vagabondes – Vagabond Stars – sees Nekfeu leave club music behind and return to the more traditional flows that earned him a loyal audience with 1995. Over interesting production and alongside gifted featured artists like Damso, Nekfeu tells stories rooted in personal experience but vague enough to be relatable to a broader audience. Individual tracks can be hit-or-miss, but as a whole the album is a new sound for the rapper that seems to synthesize his pop and hip-hop influences. Perhaps the best track on the album is “Elle Pleut” (“She Rains/Cries”). On it, Nek showcases his ability to weave a compelling story while navigating a melancholy beat that pairs ambient harmonies with a sparse drum sample that drops out for the verse, leaving him to rap almost unaccompanied. The story of a romance turned sour is told with allusions to medieval French romance and the plight of a sleepless lover, unable to view his own reflection because it reminds him of his former partner. “D’où sors-tu ; ta douceur tue,” he half-speaks, half-sings (“Where are you from; your sweetness kills”). The song’s emotional depth and sophisticated, clear production stand out on an album that rarely comes up short. Other standouts include “Takotsubo” and “Ciel Noir.”

Listen to the full album on Apple Music or Spotify .

Angry Black Men - "Riot"
Angry Blackmen was formed in early 2017. The group is comprised of two members: Quentin Branch and Brian Warren, who met on location of a music video shoot back in 2014. In December of 2016 Brian came up with the idea of the group and Quentin later designed the now iconic logo.

From 2017-2018 Angry Blackmen released a slew of singles gaining a cult-like following and being featured on popular blogs like Lyrical Lemonade, Earmilk, Elevator etc. In early 2019, Angry Blackmen released their debut EP “Talkshit!” to underground critical acclaim.

This track “Riot” is packed full of energy. In their music video for the track, Quentin and Brian, bring even more enthusiasm, knocking items off shelves in a store, breaking items, and burning the American flag, asking their audience “if they’re down to start a riot”. The duo’s smart verses, impressive delivery, and energy all make “Riot” an exciting listen.

Greentea Peng - "Downers"
Greentea Peng’s “Downers” is a beautiful, yet somber R&B track. After pressing play, the first thing you hear is this clean string sequence, which is then looped to make up part of the production.

The clean production and the strings immediately stand out, but after a couple of repetitions, The London singer comes in with a smooth chorus, showcasing her amazing voice and introducing her audience to the song’s subject matter.

She sings: “I can’t smell flowers, felt empty now for hours, lost my powers, I can’t smell the flowers, I’m sick of all these towers, done too many downers”.

You can immediately hear the sadness in her voice, detailing mental health issues and problems with antidepressants. Greentea Peng can no longer smell the roses, and is struggling with day to day life. She goes from verse to verse describing her struggles with mental health, feeling lonely, estranged from friends, and coping by getting high.

From the Greentea Peng’s amazing voice, to the incredible production, as well as the intense emotions conveyed by the lyrics of the track, “Downers” is a track that is definitely worth a listen. The track initially came out as a COLORS Studio show in June, but is now out on all streaming platforms.
