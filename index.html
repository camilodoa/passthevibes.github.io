about join us
In the modern day and age, everyday music listeners have access to millions of songs at the click of the button or tap of a screen. With this access, it’s tedious to search for new music, and easy to fall into the habit of only listening to our favorites or the top hits.

We created SleptOn as a blog for people around the world to share recently released, under-the-radar tracks and albums. Each post has a short but meaningful editorial, justifying why that artist, track or album deserves a listen.

What makes SleptOn different?

SleptOn provides a communal experience for music listeners around the globe. Anyone can contribute to the blog, all you have to do is pick one of your favorite songs that you think deserves more attention and submit a link, along with a short passage justifying your choice.

J Rick - Gone
London producer J Rick is back with “Gone”. The song came out Thursday, May 24th and continues to showcase his ability and versatility as a producer. After producing several tracks for rising UK artist Octavian, including his breakout single “Party Here”, J Rick is focusing on his own project, No Retreat, No Surrender. “Gone” is the second single for the project, after “Short”, which came out back in February. Both tracks are marked by intriguing samples of vocals and tracks layered into fluid, changing, synthy beat. “Gone” starts with a sample from J Ricks late uncle, British boxer Errol Christie, speaking on boxing as one of the only ways for Black people to make it and later flips a handful of lyrics from Blackstreet’s 1994 “Before I Let You Go”, before mixing in vocals from a viral clip of a sermon from Bishop Dag Heward-Mills. The samples work perfectly with a beat that is always changing, yet keeping the same central themes and elements. The music video is equally as exciting. The video adds to the feel of “Gone”, pairing the audio with a montage of clips of J Rick adventuring, but also the wide variety of other people, places, and sights that were a part of his trip to Egypt. The track and the video stand out as another creative expression from a talented producer, trying to showcase his own individual artistry as opposed to taking a backseat to a vocalist. “Gone” is a funky, experimental track that adds to the growing hype around J Rick, and his upcoming project.
